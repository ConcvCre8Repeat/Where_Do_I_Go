Baserunning Fundamentals
These Rules Never Change
Run HardAlways run fast. Even if you think you might get out, errors happen all the time. You are not out until called out.
Through 1stOn a ground ball, run fast through 1st base! Never slow down before the bag. Step on the front of the base as you cross it.
Eyes UpAs you round any base, find your base coach immediately. They see things you can't. Trust them.
Tag UpFly Ball or Pop Up? Get back to your base and wait. Tag and go if it's caught. Run if it drops.
Go HomeIf the coach sends you home — do not stop. Run all the way through the plate.
Picture ItBefore the pitch, ask yourself: "If the ball is hit here, I go here."
Stay ReadyOn your toes, alert and ready. The play is not over until the pitcher has the ball in the circle.
Stay Smart
Know the OutsBefore every pitch, know how many outs there are. 0 or 1 outs: be careful, tag up on fly balls. 2 outs: run hard on the hit.
Know the ScoreBehind? Be aggressive. Winning by a lot? Be smart and don't give away outs on the bases.
Force Out vs. Tag Out — Know the Difference
Am I Forced?Simple check: is there a runner on every base behind me? If yes, you are forced to run. If no, you can make a decision.
Force OutYou MUST run. The defense just needs to get the ball to your base before you. Run hard.
Tag OutNot forced? The defense has to physically tag you. You can stay, go, or make them chase you.
Tag TipDon't run into the glove. Stop short, fake them out, force a rundown. Errors happen.

Base Coach Signals
1B — First Base Coach SignalsRun through
or keep going
✋ Stop at 1BRun through the bag. Turn toward foul territory and return to 1st. Turning toward 2nd risks a tag out.
🔄 Keep GoingBall hit hard to outfield? Round the bag toward 2nd. Look to the 3B coach on your way.
👆 BackToo risky? Get back to 1st immediately. Don't give them an easy out.
3B — Third Base Coach SignalsThis coach
is your eyes!
👀 Look!Find 3B Coach with your eyes. Running from 1st to 2nd? Look to 3B Coach for stop or go.
🙌 Hands UpSTOP at 2nd or 3rd. Plant your foot on the bag. Do not go to next base.
🌀 WindmillGO GO GO — no stopping. Head down, run as fast as you can. Run Run Run.
👇 PointingPrepare for a close play. Get there. Slide. Get low, get safe, stay on the bag.

Baserunning Scenarios
BR-1Runner on 1B — Ground Ball — Force!0 or 1 outs
Runner 1BRun hard to 2nd. Don't stop to watch the play.
BatterRun through 1st. Run hard even if the 1B fields it clean.
Forced RunnersIf forced from 2nd or 3rd — don't stop to watch. Run hard to next base or home.
BR-2Runner on 2B — Ground Ball — Read!0 or 1 outs
Right Side HitRight side (2B or 1B) → GO! Watch the 3B coach. Usually waved home when ball is thrown to 1st.
Left Side HitLeft side (SS or 3B) → wait and read. They may throw to 3rd. Watch the 3B coach.
BR-3Runner on 3B — Ground Ball — Go Home!0 or 1 outs
Runner 3BWatch the ball hit the ground, then go on the coach's signal. Throw to 1st is your green light home.
2 OutsWith 2 outs, run on almost ANY contact. The inning ends if the batter is out anyway.
BR-4Fly Ball — Less Than 2 OutsTag up!
All RunnersGet back to your base and freeze. Caught → tag then decide. Dropped → run immediately.
Runner 3BDeep fly ball caught? Tag up and go home. The outfield throw is long — you can score.
Shallow FlyCaught close to infield? Get back and stay there. Don't give an easy out.
BR-5Fly Ball — 2 OutsRun on contact
All RunnersWith 2 outs, run the moment the ball is hit. If caught, inning ends anyway. If dropped, you're already running.
BR-6Runner on 2B — Hit to Left FieldScoring chance
Runner 2BGo on contact. Round 3rd hard and watch your 3B coach for the wave home.
Watch ForLF could throw to 3rd — be ready to stop or slide if the coach signals. Don't run through a tag you can't beat.
BR-7Infield Pop Up — All RunnersFreeze!
All RunnersGet back to your base and freeze. An infielder can catch and throw for a double out.
If DroppedBall hits the ground — go immediately if forced.
2 OutsWith 2 outs, run the moment the ball is hit. Inning ends anyway if caught.
BR-8Runner on 1B — Hit to Right FieldScore from 1st!
Runner 1BRF has the hardest throw home. Round 2nd hard and look at the 3B coach. A clean RF hit almost always gets you to 3rd.
Forced RunnersOn 2B or 3B — RUN! A clean RF hit almost always gets you home from 2B or 3B.
BR-9Hit Down the 3B LineExtra bases!
BatterThis can be a double if you run hard. Look to 1B coach — round 1st and go!
Runner 1BBall down the line gives you time. You may get to 3rd or score. Only stop if the 3B coach says stop.
Runner 2B/3BA hard hit down the 3B line will usually send you home. Run hard — look to 3B coach.
BR-10Overthrow to 1st or 3rdFree bases!
All RunnersOverthrown? Keep running — take 1 additional base. Don't stop unless the coach signals.
Stay AlertEven after reaching safely — a second overthrow can happen. Stay ready.

Smart Batting Habits
Hitting FUNdamentals — Every At Bat
See ItWatch the ball all the way to the bat. Not the pitcher, not the fielders — eyes on the ball the whole time.
Swing FastYour job is to hit the ball somewhere. Hard contact anywhere creates opportunities.
After ContactDrop the bat and sprint immediately. Don't flip it, don't watch. First base is 60 feet — run.
Look to 1B CoachThe moment you run toward 1st, look to 1B Coach. Run through or head to 2nd.
2 StrikesReset, breathe. See the ball, trust your hands, and swing. A groundball anywhere beats a strikeout.
Strike Out?Even the best strike out. Keep swinging, have fun, try your best. You'll get it next time.
Next Level Batting
Know the Count0–1 strikes: be selective. 2 strikes: protect the plate — shorten your swing and put the ball in play.
Know the RunnersBefore you step in, know where every runner is. A hit to the right spot could drive in a run.
